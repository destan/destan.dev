Turkish citizens do NOT require employers to be recognized sponsors

Unlike other non-EU nationals, Turkish citizens can work for ANY Dutch employer with just an employment contract.

Official Policy

According to the Immigration and Naturalisation Service (IND):

Normally, only recognised sponsors can apply for these residence permits. This rule does not apply to Turkish employees. You can also submit the application yourself.

Source: IND - Turkish citizens and living in the Netherlands

What This Means for You

  • No sponsor recognition needed - Your company doesn't need IND sponsor status
  • No sponsor obligations - No administrative duties or reporting requirements
  • Standard employment contract sufficient - Regular employment contract allows permit application
  • Applies to all positions - Including highly skilled migrant and researcher roles

Legal Basis

EU-Turkey Association Agreement (1963)

This exemption exists due to the EU-Turkey Association Agreement (1963), which grants Turkish citizens special rights in EU member states.

An exception applies to highly-skilled migrants and researchers with Turkish nationality. And recognition is not mandatory

Source: IND - Apply for recognition as sponsor
